The Timeless Allure of Hercule Poirot

By Harry Stevens, Rohan Naahar, and Supriya Sharma
25 November, 2017

With the release this week of Murder on the Orient Express, starring Kenneth Branagh as famous fictional detective Hercule Poirot, HT takes a look at the mustachioed sleuth’s enduring appeal.

Murder on the Orient Express is the most adapted Poirot book

Between 1920 and 1975, Agatha Christie wrote 33 books starring Hercule Poirot, who uses the “little grey cells” in his brain to solve the most perplexing murders.

Each of the books has been adapted at least once into a TV series or film. Murder on the Orient Express has been adapted the most — five times, including a forthcoming release starring Kenneth Branagh.
